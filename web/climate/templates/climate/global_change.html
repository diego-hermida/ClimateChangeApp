{% trans "What are RPCs?" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}

Climate predictions are based on climate models, which stimulate aspects such as the temperature of the atmosphere and the oceans, rainfall, winds, cloud formations, ocean currents and ice masses. In addition, factors of human activity such as economic and demographic growth, lifestyles, energy use, technology and climate policies are taken into account.

To obtain the projections of the parameters to be evaluated (concentration of greenhouse gases, average temperature of the planet, etc.), climate models are used using information present in scenarios, known as Representative Concentration Pathways or RPCs. These describe alternative trajectories for the parameters of interest, and cover up to the year 2100.

{% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "RPC 2.6" %}
  • {% trans "CO₂ emissions: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} They reach a maximum in 2020 (and are reduced to 0 in 2080), with a maximum concentration of 440 ppm. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% trans "Population: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Around 9,000 million in 2050. Economic development is high. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% trans "Energy: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Abandonment of oil as a fuel, replaced by other fossil fuels and renewable energies. {% endblocktrans %}
{% blocktrans trimmed %} This is the only scenario that meets the objective of maintaining the temperature of the Earth below 2ºC above pre-industrial levels. {% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "RPC 4.5" %}
  • {% trans "CO₂ emissions: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Levels 50% higher than the 2000 levels in 2050. Towards 2080 they stabilize at the levels of 2000. Concentration of 520 ppm in 2070, increasing slightly. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% trans "Population: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Economic and demographic growth slightly lower than that of RPC 2.6. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% trans "Energy: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Consumption of oil practically constant until 2100. Nuclear and renewable energies as secondary sources. {% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "RPC 6.0" %}
  • {% trans "CO₂ emissions: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Emissions 100% higher than 2000 levels in 2060, with ever increasing concentrations -reducing the pace at the end of the century-, reaching 620 ppm in 2100. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% trans "Population: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Growth up to 10,000 million inhabitants, with low GDP. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% trans "Energy: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Peak energy by 2060, reaching the level of RPC 2.6 in 2100. Consumptions similar to RPC 4.5 with minor importance secondary energies. {% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "RPC 8.5" %}
  • {% trans "CO₂ emissions: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Emissions 375% higher than the current levels in 2100. Concentration of 950 ppm in 2100 and rising during minimum, 100 more years. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% trans "Population: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} High population growth, up to 12,000 in 2100. Social inequality. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% trans "Energy: " %} {% blocktrans trimmed %} Increased energy consumption throughout the century, three times higher than the 2000 levels. Use of oil until 2070 and coal until 2100. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Consequences" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}

To meet the objective of keeping the global temperature below 2°C above pre-industrial levels, it is necessary to limit the emissions to a maximum of 3650 GtCO₂ (giga-tons of CO₂), half of which were already emitted towards 2011. The RPC 2.6 is the only scenario that ensures the fullfillment of this objective.

Therefore, of the RPC 2.6 scenario is not met, the consequences will be negative at multiple levels:

  • The Arctic is likely to be entirely disappeared before 2100.
  • The climate will become more extreme in all regions.
  • Heat waves will occur more frequently.
  • A considerable percentage of terrestrial and marine species will be unable to adapt and will increase their risk of extinction.
  • The risk of flooding and erosion of coastal areas will be increased.
  • The quantity and quality of drinking water will be reduced; above all, in tropical and subtropical regions.
  • The spread of diseases by vectors, water and food is likely to be increased.
{% endblocktrans %}
